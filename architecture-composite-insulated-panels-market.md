<p><p>Composite insulated panels are building materials that consist of two layers of metal or other materials with an insulating core sandwiched in between. The market for these panels can be classified into different types based on the core material used. EPS panels use expanded polystyrene as the insulating core, PUR/PIR panels use polyurethane or polyisocyanurate foam, mineral/glass wool panels use mineral or glass fibers for insulation. Other types of composite insulated panels may use different materials or combinations thereof for the core. The choice of panel type depends on factors such as insulation requirements, cost, and intended application.</p></p>

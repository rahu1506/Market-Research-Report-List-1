<p><p>The Network-Attached Storage (NAS) market comprises two types: scale-up and scale-out. In the scale-up type, the storage capacity and processing power are increased by adding more resources to a single node. This is suitable for small and medium-sized businesses with predictable storage needs. On the other hand, in the scale-out type, multiple nodes are connected, allowing storage and processing resources to be added horizontally. This caters to large enterprises and organizations that require flexible and scalable storage solutions to accommodate unpredictable data growth.</p></p>
